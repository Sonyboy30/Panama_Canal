Engineering and Labor

Under Chief Engineers John Stevens and George Goethals, the United States redesigned the canal using an ingenious lock system. Over 75,000 workers—many from the Caribbean—endured harsh, segregated conditions while carving the massive Culebra Cut through Panama’s mountains.
