In 1904, the United States took control of the failed French project. Engineers like John Stevens and George Goethals turned chaos into coordination, using lock systems, steel gates, and electric power to conquer the isthmus. Workers excavated 240 million cubic yards of earth, completing the canal in 1914 and forever transforming global trade.
