Historical Background

The dream of linking the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans through Panama dates back to the 1500s. By the late 19th century, French engineer Ferdinand de Lesseps, builder of the Suez Canal, began the first construction effort. However, the project collapsed due to disease, landslides, and financial ruin.

In 1903, the United States under President Theodore Roosevelt took over construction after supporting Panama’s independence. By 1914, the Panama Canal was completed—forever transforming world trade and symbolizing a new era of American power and engineering excellence.
