Enduring Impact

Completed in 1914, the Panama Canal revolutionized global commerce and cemented the U.S. as a world power. It remains vital to trade today, handling over 12,000 ships per year. Its story embodies both the triumph and contradiction of the Progressive Era.

SS Ancon, first ship through the Panama Canal, 1914 — U.S. Navy Archives
“The canal was not just a triumph of engineering—it was a statement of power.” — National Archives, 2022
