Commemorative Plaque

This plaque honors the over 75,000 men and women who labored to build the Panama Canal between 1904 and 1914. Workers came from more than forty countries—many from the Caribbean—facing grueling tropical conditions, disease, and long hours. Their courage and skill turned one of the world’s most challenging engineering dreams into reality. The Canal not only transformed global trade but also marked a turning point in U.S. influence abroad, symbolizing both human progress and the complexities of imperial ambition. May their perseverance remind future generations that even the grandest achievements are built by the hands of many.
