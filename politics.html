Diplomacy and Expansion

The canal symbolized both progress and power. Roosevelt’s “Big Stick” diplomacy secured control of the Canal Zone after the U.S. supported Panama’s independence from Colombia in 1903. The project strengthened America’s naval mobility and global dominance.

President Theodore Roosevelt visiting the Canal, 1906 — Library of Congress

Timeline of Key Events

1903
Panama declares independence with U.S. support; the Hay–Bunau-Varilla Treaty grants the U.S. control of the Canal Zone.
1904
Official U.S. construction of the canal begins under President Theodore Roosevelt.
1906
Roosevelt visits Panama, becoming the first sitting U.S. president to travel abroad while in office.
1914
The Panama Canal officially opens, linking the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
