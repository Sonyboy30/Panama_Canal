President Theodore Roosevelt viewed the canal as America’s gateway to global influence. After supporting Panama’s independence from Colombia in 1903, the U.S. gained control of the Canal Zone through the Hay–Bunau–Varilla Treaty. Roosevelt’s 1906 visit to the site made him the first sitting U.S. president to travel abroad and symbolized the nation’s growing reach.
